Processing in Depth

Processors

  • Intel 4th Generation CPU (Codename: Haswell)

    CPU is short for the Central Processing Unit and handles all of the computing. An average CPU these days consist of about 2 or 4 cores and about 6 or 8 megabytes of L3 Cache.

  • NVIDIA GK110 GPU Graphics Card

    GPU is short for Graphics Processing Unit and does all of the graphical image rendering. It consists of thousands of cores that work together to render an image.

Controllers

  • Audio Control Board

    This audio control board does the encoding and decoding of audio data and splits and sends the data to separate channels.

  • 6-Axis Control Board

    This 6-axis control board calculates and controls accelerometer data and sends that to the CPU.

Storage

  • SSD (Solid State Drive)

    An SSD (Solid State Drive) is currently the fastest form of permanent data storage for the consumer. It is made of NAND flash chips and a controller. SSD do not have the bottleneck of moving parts but does have the bottleneck of its data communication interface such as SATA 3 (6GB/s). The only down side to an SSD is its limited write cycles. SSDs have a cost per GB ratio of about $0.75:GB, the largest consumer SSD currently available is 1TB.

  • HDD (Hard Disk Drive)

    A HDD (Hard Disk Drive) is a slower permanent storage medium compared to an SSD but is cheaper and can support larger capacities up to 4TB. HDD have unlimited read and write cycles but consists of moving parts which are a major failure point compared to SSDs, but HDDs have a price per GB ratio of about $0.05 cents:GB which is much cheaper than an SSD.
